Yaman Parasher

I am a first year Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degree Student in Photonic Integrated Circuits, Sensors and Networks (PIXNET) at, TeCIP, Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna (SSSA), Pisa, Italy. The PIXNET Consortium assigned me the Mobility path no. 6 - “Theory and Techniques for Photonic Integrated Devices” curriculum, in which I will be spending my first year at TeCIP, Scuola Superiore Sant’Anna, second year (3rd Semester) at Aston Institute of Photonic Technologies, Aston University, UK and the last or the 4th Semester at Photonics Center Osaka University, Japan.

Before coming to TeCIP, SSSA, I was working as a Project Associate at Delhi Technological University (DTU), New Delhi, India in the Optical Communication & Photonics Research Group of Prof. Dr. Gurjit Kaur .Here, I worked primarily on the use of signal processing, communication theory, and optical techniques in designing high capacity optical multiuser/multichannel systems and networks. Apart from this, I was also involved in the design, & modeling of a large class of photonic devices, like vertical-cavity surface-emitting lasers, and photonic crystal devices, to enable further optical communication advances.

Apart from this, I have also been a research intern in the PhotonicsNanostructures and Devices Lab at National Chung Cheng University (CCU), Taiwan under guidance of Prof. Dr. Guo-En Chang .Here, I developed a highly reflective GeSn based resonant cavity structure in the SWIR region using COMSOL for group IV photonics devices. Here I’ve also developed novice level expertise in working with fabrication processes like Photolithography and RIE(Reactive Ion Etching) for various MEMS-based applications.

Experience encompasses working on various in lab optical system design software tools & workbench alongwith expertise in modeling, design, & simulation of advance photonic integrated circuits. Published research papers in reputed Science Cited Indexed (SCI) journals such as Elsevier, Springer and international conference of Optical Society of America (OSA), LAOP-2018 in Peru.
